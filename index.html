Goals of This Project:

  1. Determine which performance metrics are determinats of winning races. I plan to utilize different datasets on results, pitstops, and qualifying rounds to determine correlations between these metrics and race results
  2. Determine if performance or location is an indicator of economic success of a Formuala One Race Weekend. I hope to add in datasets on driver salary, profit from each race, team budgets, and other economic indicators to determine if money truly is an important factor in Formula One

Data Sets Overview:

  1. circuits.csv : This dataset gives an overview of the different circuits within Formula One and their geographical information. A circuit is the track where a Formula One race occurs

  2. constructor_results.csv : This dataset summarizes how each constructor did at each race and how many points they received.

  3. constructor_standings.csv : This dataset highlights how each constructor did at specific races as well as the points their team received for said standings

  4. constructors.csv : This dataset gives information on the different constructors within Formula One, their specific ID, and nationality. A constructor in Formula One is a company that designs and builds the race cars. Each team has a unique constructor

  5. driver_standings.csv : This dataset illustrates the outcomes of each race. It shows how each driver did at specific races and how many points were awarded

  6. drivers.csv : This dataset gives detailed information on all Formula One drivers for the past 70+ years. Within this dataset, there is information on nationality as well as age.

  7. lap_times.csv : This dataset details each driver and how they performed each lap of every race. Each row signifies how a specific driver performed for each lap. We are given position after completing the lap as well as the time of lap

  8. pit_stops.csv : This dataset provides information about all pitstops within the races. The pitstop dataset provides detailed information on stop length, what lap the stop occurred, and which driver was stopping.

  9. qualifying.csv : To determine grid placement on race day, drivers compete in qualifying rounds on Saturday. This dataset details how each driver did at qualifiers, the overall time for each lap, and the placement earned for Sunday's race

  10. races.csv : This dataset gives specific IDs to each race within Formula One between 1950-2022. The dataset has information on the circuit, what date the race was on, and even what time the race began

  11. results.csv : This dataset summarizes pertinent information regarding the results of each Formula One race. Within the dataset, we can see where each driver started, where they finished and their fastest lap time

  12. seasons.csv : This dataset provides a quick Wikipedia link for pertinent information on each Formula One Season.

  13. sprint_results.csv : Sprint races are new to Formula One. They are not offered at every race but only typically at 'big' races. Only 1/3 the time of a normal race, the drivers compete in a 'mock race' to earn more points for their team and learn the track. This dataset captures important timing and placing metrics for the recent sprint races.

  14. status.csv : This is a foreign key table that provides text explanations for the different race statuses

qualifying dataframe¶

How do qualifying rounds work in Formula One?

There are three qualifying rounds for each race weekend in Formula One. These rounds take place typically over two days prior to the race on Sunday. In the first round, all twenty Formula One drivers participate. The driver's race one lap individually and attempt to recieve the fastest time. Those in the top fifteen will move on to the second round of qualifiers. Those with the bottom five times, will start race day in the last five positions on the grid. In the second round of qualifiers, drivers drive one lap, once again aiming for the faastest time. Those with the fastest ten times will move on to the third round of qualifiers. Those who had the bottom five race times in Q2 will be placed in spots 10-15 on the grid for Sunday's race. The final qualifying round determines the order of the drivers who will be in positions 1-10 on race day.

How should this information shape our data?

Since not all drivers will participate in all three rounds of qualifying, I am going to create dummy variables that will indicate (based on their success in qualifiers) whether a driver will race in the top ten, middle five, or bottom five.
